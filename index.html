Some stuff about aquaponics

Hydroponics

The domestication of fish for food in an enclosed space

Aquaculture

The process of growing plants without soil

Tom's farm is based in Lake Geneva, Wisconsin and specilizes in providing fresh organic produce and fish through aquaponic agriculture. This process combines fish based aquaculture with plant based hydroponics in a sustainable, environmentally friendly system.

Aquaponics is a sustainable agriculture technique that provides free and safe nutrients to both the fish and the produce in a closed system. Our system produces delicious fresh produce and fish without harmful pesticides, ferterlizer runoff, and uses 90% less water than traditional agriculture.

How it works

  1. The fish live in large tanks
  2. Waste from the fish is siphoned off and broken down by bacteria
  3. The processed fish waste is pumped into the plants root system
  4. Plants produce their own waste, which settles towards the fish tanks
  5. The fish eat the plant waste
